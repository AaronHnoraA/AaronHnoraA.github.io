# 把 Tensor Isomorphism 归约到 Tensor Automorphism

# 执行摘要

本文目标有两个：

1. 给出一个从 Tensor Isomorphism $TI$ 到 Tensor Automorphism $TA$ 的可执行、可写入论文的证明框架；
2. 把这件事拆成小组可并行推进的工作包。

本文的主线不是“把 $TI$ 直接压成最弱版本的 $TA$ 判定问题”，而是先区分三类 automorphism oracle：

1. 返回整个自同构群；
2. 返回自同构群的一组生成元；
3. 只判断是否存在非平凡自同构。

对前两类 oracle，我们可以给出清晰的构造、正确性证明与复杂性分析。

对第三类 oracle，一般情形下它不够强。原因是：

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(A\oplus B)
$$

中的非平凡元素可能只来自 $A$ 或 $B$ 的内部对称性，而不一定来自交换两个 summands 的 switching automorphism。

因此，若只使用判定型 $TA$ oracle，则需要额外的刚性化 gadget 或 promise 条件。

本文的结构性主线如下。

给定两个三阶张量

$$
A \in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
B \in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B,
$$

先把它们限制到各自的 minimal support spaces 上，使其成为 concise / non-degenerate 的形式。然后构造 direct sum tensor

$$
S := A\oplus B.
$$

核心命题是：

$$
A \cong B
\Longleftrightarrow
S=A\oplus B
\text{ has a switching automorphism}.
$$

这里的 switching automorphism 是指一个自同构

$$
(P,Q,R)\in \operatorname{Aut}(S)
$$

同时交换三条 tensor legs 上的两个 summands：

$$
P(U_A)=U_B,
\qquad
P(U_B)=U_A,
$$

$$
Q(V_A)=V_B,
\qquad
Q(V_B)=V_A,
$$

$$
R(W_A)=W_B,
\qquad
R(W_B)=W_A.
$$

这个 iff 本身不需要有限域，也不需要预先假设 $A$ 与 $B$ indecomposable。它只是一个 block-switching 与 restriction maps 的双向证明。

真正的算法难点不在这个 iff，而在于：

$$
\text{如何从 } TA \text{ oracle 的输出中检测 switch?}
$$

如果 oracle 返回整个群，检测 switch 是直接枚举问题。

如果 oracle 返回生成元，则必须强调：

#+begin important
不能写成 “the generating set contains a switch”。

正确说法是：

$$
\text{the group generated by the returned generators contains a switch}.
$$

也就是说，生成集本身未必字面含有 switching element；switch 可能只出现在生成群中。
#+end important

为了把 Strassen / Krull--Schmidt 语言补全到论文可用程度，本文还整理 direct sum、indecomposable、minimal support spaces、centroid / center 与 idempotent 的关系。

在本文中，Krull--Schmidt / centroid / idempotent 语言承担的是第二层职责：

1. 解释为什么 direct-sum decomposition 可以由 idempotents 控制；
2. 为算法检测 switch 提供 canonical decomposition route；
3. 帮助处理 refined decomposition 与 indecomposable summands 的唯一性。

但核心 switching criterion 本身不依赖这套理论。

# 引言与优先文献

把“张量同构”放到复杂度与群作用的交叉点去看，已经形成了一条相对清晰的文献链。

Strassen 在 1987 年把 bilinear complexity、tensor、restriction、degeneration、matrix multiplication exponent 放进同一个框架中。这仍然是今天最自然的语言起点。

之后，环同构、环自同构与多项式等价问题被证明与 automorphism / isomorphism 问题紧密相关。Kayal--Saxena 的工作以及 Agrawal--Saxena 的综述特别强调：在 basis representation 下，有限环的 automorphism / isomorphism 问题有明确复杂度上界；图同构可以归约到 ring isomorphism，而 ring isomorphism 又与 cubic form equivalence 发生连接。

在复杂度论一侧，Grochow--Qiao 把 tensors、groups、polynomials、algebras 的一大类同构问题统一为 $TI$-complete 现象，明确提出 Tensor Isomorphism 作为一个稳健的等价类。后续工作又继续把这条链扩展到线性长度 gadget 与交换环情形。

在分解与 automorphism 的算法侧，Wilson 发展了 direct product / Remak decomposition 的群论算法框架，并把 bilinear-map 观点放到 central decomposition、pseudo-isometry 与相关 algebraic invariant 上。Brooksbank--Maglione--Wilson 又继续推进了 tensor 的 automorphism / derivation / densor 路线。

最后，在本文最需要的 “direct sum 与 indecomposable 如何由 centroid / center 控制” 这个点上，有两条特别重要的文献线索：

1. Huang--Lu--Ye--Zhang 对非退化 multilinear forms / multilinear maps 的中心代数理论；
2. Canino 等人在一般 Segre / Segre--Veronese 格式下对 centroid、refined direct-sum decomposition 及 locality 判别的系统处理。

前者给出“中心代数交换、正交幂等元对应 direct sum、indecomposable 分解唯一”的清晰原型；后者则把这件事真正推到一般 Segre 格式张量。

# 形式化定义与精确模型

#+begin definition Tensor Isomorphism
Let

$$
A \in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
B \in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B
$$

be third-order tensors over a field $k$.

We say that $A$ and $B$ are tensor-isomorphic, written

$$
A\cong B,
$$

if there exist linear isomorphisms

$$
P:U_A\to U_B,
\qquad
Q:V_A\to V_B,
\qquad
R:W_A\to W_B
$$

such that

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)A=B.
$$

The Tensor Isomorphism problem asks whether such a triple $(P,Q,R)$ exists.
#+end definition

#+begin definition Tensor Automorphism
Let

$$
T\in U\otimes V\otimes W.
$$

The automorphism group of $T$ is

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(T)
=
\{
(P,Q,R)\in
\operatorname{GL}(U)\times \operatorname{GL}(V)\times \operatorname{GL}(W)
:
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)T=T
\}.
$$

The phrase Tensor Automorphism is not a single canonical decision problem. In this note we distinguish several oracle models, depending on whether the oracle returns the whole group, generators, or only a yes/no answer about nontrivial automorphisms.
#+end definition

#+begin definition Minimal Support Spaces
Let

$$
T\in U\otimes V\otimes W.
$$

Define

$$
U_{\min}(T)
=
\operatorname{span}
\{
(\operatorname{id}_U\otimes \varphi\otimes \psi)(T)
:
\varphi\in V^*,
\psi\in W^*
\}
\subseteq U.
$$

Similarly define

$$
V_{\min}(T)\subseteq V
$$

and

$$
W_{\min}(T)\subseteq W
$$

by contracting along the other two pairs of tensor factors.

If

$$
U_{\min}(T)=U,
\qquad
V_{\min}(T)=V,
\qquad
W_{\min}(T)=W,
$$

then $T$ is called concise, or non-degenerate in the support sense.
#+end definition

#+begin note
Some authors use “non-degenerate” in a stronger or slightly different sense. To avoid ambiguity, “concise” or “support-nondegenerate” is the safer terminology here.
#+end note

#+begin definition Direct Sum of Tensors
Let

$$
A\in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
B\in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B.
$$

Inside

$$
(U_A\oplus U_B)
\otimes
(V_A\oplus V_B)
\otimes
(W_A\oplus W_B),
$$

we define

$$
A\oplus B
$$

to be the tensor whose only nonzero pure blocks are

$$
A\in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
B\in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
A\oplus B=A+B
$$

under the natural block embeddings, with all mixed blocks equal to zero.
#+end definition

#+begin warning Direct sum is not merely ambient vector-space direct sum
The ambient spaces decompose as

$$
U=U_A\oplus U_B,
\qquad
V=V_A\oplus V_B,
\qquad
W=W_A\oplus W_B.
$$

But the tensor itself must have no mixed components such as

$$
U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_B
$$

or

$$
U_A\otimes V_B\otimes W_A.
$$

Only the two pure blocks

$$
U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B
$$

are allowed to appear.
#+end warning

#+begin definition Switching Automorphism
Let

$$
S=A\oplus B.
$$

An automorphism

$$
(P,Q,R)\in \operatorname{Aut}(S)
$$

is called a switching automorphism, relative to the displayed two-block decomposition, if

$$
P(U_A)=U_B,
\qquad
P(U_B)=U_A,
$$

$$
Q(V_A)=V_B,
\qquad
Q(V_B)=V_A,
$$

and

$$
R(W_A)=W_B,
\qquad
R(W_B)=W_A.
$$
#+end definition

#+begin definition Indecomposable Tensor
A nonzero tensor $T$ is called indecomposable if, after restricting to its minimal support spaces, it cannot be written as a nontrivial tensor direct sum.
#+end definition

# TA oracle 模型

| $TA$ oracle 模型 | 输入输出 | 对 $TI$ 到 $TA$ 归约的影响 |
|---|---|---|
| $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ | 输入 $T$，返回 $\operatorname{Aut}(T)$ 的全部元素。有限域时这个模型才比较现实。 | 最直接：逐个检测是否存在 switch。 |
| $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ | 输入 $T$，返回 $\operatorname{Aut}(T)$ 的一组生成元。 | 可行，但必须补矩阵群或置换群后处理；生成集本身未必含 switch。 |

# 主要定理与证明结构

#+begin theorem Minimal Support Spaces Are Intrinsic
Let

$$
T\in U\otimes V\otimes W.
$$

Then:

1. $T$ lies in its minimal support spaces:

   $$
   T\in
   U_{\min}(T)
   \otimes
   V_{\min}(T)
   \otimes
   W_{\min}(T).
   $$

2. If

   $$
   T\in U'\otimes V'\otimes W'
   $$

   for subspaces

   $$
   U'\subseteq U,
   \qquad
   V'\subseteq V,
   \qquad
   W'\subseteq W,
   $$

   then

   $$
   U_{\min}(T)\subseteq U',
   \qquad
   V_{\min}(T)\subseteq V',
   \qquad
   W_{\min}(T)\subseteq W'.
   $$

3. If

   $$
   T'=(P\otimes Q\otimes R)T,
   $$

   where $P,Q,R$ are linear isomorphisms, then

   $$
   U_{\min}(T')=P(U_{\min}(T)),
   $$

   $$
   V_{\min}(T')=Q(V_{\min}(T)),
   $$

   and

   $$
   W_{\min}(T')=R(W_{\min}(T)).
   $$
#+end theorem

#+begin theorem Switching Criterion
Let

$$
A\in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A
$$

and

$$
B\in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B
$$

be tensors, each viewed on its own minimal support spaces.

Let

$$
S:=A\oplus B.
$$

Then the following are equivalent:

1. $A\cong B$.

2. $S$ has a switching automorphism relative to the displayed decomposition

   $$
   S=A\oplus B.
   $$
#+end theorem

#+begin proposition Krull--Schmidt type refined decomposition
For concise tensors in the general Segre format, the centroid is a commutative unital algebra. The finest direct-sum decomposition is controlled by maximal ideals / primitive idempotents of this centroid. In particular, a tensor is indecomposable if and only if the corresponding centroid is local.

For equal-space multilinear forms, this statement can be proved using the center algebra. For general Segre-format tensors, one should use the centroid version.
#+end proposition

#+begin summary Why the main proof does not need indecomposability at the start
The core iff

$$
A\cong B
\Longleftrightarrow
A\oplus B
\text{ has a switching automorphism}
$$

is only a block-switching statement. It does not require $A$ and $B$ to be indecomposable.

Indecomposable / Krull--Schmidt theory serves a second-layer role:

1. it gives canonical decomposition language for detecting switches;
2. it explains why idempotents and centroids naturally appear;
3. it helps control repeated isomorphism types and refined direct-sum decompositions.
#+end summary

# 完整证明与证明骨架

#+begin lemma Minimality of support spaces
For

$$
T\in U\otimes V\otimes W,
$$

the subspace

$$
U_{\min}(T)
$$

is the smallest subspace $U'\subseteq U$ such that

$$
T\in U'\otimes V\otimes W.
$$

The analogous statements hold for $V_{\min}(T)$ and $W_{\min}(T)$.
#+end lemma

#+begin proof
We prove the statement for the first tensor factor.

Suppose

$$
T\in U'\otimes V\otimes W
$$

for some subspace $U'\subseteq U$.

For every

$$
\varphi\in V^*,
\qquad
\psi\in W^*,
$$

the contraction

$$
(\operatorname{id}_U\otimes \varphi\otimes \psi)(T)
$$

lies in $U'$.

Therefore

$$
U_{\min}(T)\subseteq U'.
$$

So $U_{\min}(T)$ is contained in every possible first support space $U'$.

Conversely, by construction $U_{\min}(T)$ is spanned by all first-leg contractions of $T$. Hence $T$ can be expressed using only vectors from $U_{\min}(T)$ in the first tensor factor. Thus

$$
T\in U_{\min}(T)\otimes V\otimes W.
$$

The same argument applies to the second and third tensor factors. Iterating the argument gives

$$
T\in
U_{\min}(T)
\otimes
V_{\min}(T)
\otimes
W_{\min}(T).
$$
#+end proof

#+begin proof Minimal support spaces are invariant under isomorphism
Suppose

$$
T'=(P\otimes Q\otimes R)T.
$$

For every

$$
\varphi'\in (V')^*,
\qquad
\psi'\in (W')^*,
$$

we have

$$
(\operatorname{id}\otimes \varphi'\otimes \psi')(T')
=
P
\left(
(\operatorname{id}\otimes (\varphi'\circ Q)\otimes(\psi'\circ R))(T)
\right).
$$

Therefore every first-leg contraction of $T'$ lies in

$$
P(U_{\min}(T)).
$$

Hence

$$
U_{\min}(T')\subseteq P(U_{\min}(T)).
$$

Applying the same argument to

$$
T=(P^{-1}\otimes Q^{-1}\otimes R^{-1})T'
$$

gives the reverse inclusion. Therefore

$$
U_{\min}(T')=P(U_{\min}(T)).
$$

The same proof gives

$$
V_{\min}(T')=Q(V_{\min}(T))
$$

and

$$
W_{\min}(T')=R(W_{\min}(T)).
$$
#+end proof

#+begin remark Writing significance
This theorem tells us that any formal proof involving $TI$ should first restrict the input tensors to their minimal support spaces.

Otherwise, ambient zero directions can create fake automorphisms or fake block switches that obscure the statement.
#+end remark

#+begin lemma A switching automorphism gives an isomorphism
Let

$$
S=A\oplus B.
$$

If

$$
(P,Q,R)\in \operatorname{Aut}(S)
$$

is a switching automorphism, then

$$
A\cong B.
$$
#+end lemma

#+begin proof
Since $P,Q,R$ exchange the two summands on each tensor factor, we have

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)A
\in
U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B,
$$

and

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)B
\in
U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A.
$$

On the other hand,

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)S=S.
$$

Since

$$
S=A+B
$$

with $A$ and $B$ lying in two disjoint pure blocks, comparing the two pure block components gives

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)A=B
$$

and

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)B=A.
$$

Therefore

$$
(P|_{U_A},Q|_{V_A},R|_{W_A})
$$

is an isomorphism from $A$ to $B$.

Hence

$$
A\cong B.
$$
#+end proof

#+begin lemma An isomorphism gives a switching automorphism
If

$$
A\cong B,
$$

then

$$
S:=A\oplus B
$$

has a switching automorphism.
#+end lemma

#+begin proof
Assume

$$
A\cong B.
$$

Then there exist linear isomorphisms

$$
p:U_A\to U_B,
\qquad
q:V_A\to V_B,
\qquad
r:W_A\to W_B
$$

such that

$$
(p\otimes q\otimes r)A=B.
$$

Define

$$
P:U_A\oplus U_B\to U_A\oplus U_B
$$

by

$$
P|_{U_A}=p,
\qquad
P|_{U_B}=p^{-1}.
$$

Similarly define

$$
Q|_{V_A}=q,
\qquad
Q|_{V_B}=q^{-1},
$$

and

$$
R|_{W_A}=r,
\qquad
R|_{W_B}=r^{-1}.
$$

Then $P,Q,R$ are invertible and exchange the two blocks on each tensor factor.

Moreover,

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)A=B
$$

and

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)B=A.
$$

Therefore

$$
(P\otimes Q\otimes R)(A+B)=A+B.
$$

Thus

$$
(P,Q,R)\in \operatorname{Aut}(S).
$$

Since it exchanges the two blocks on each leg, it is a switching automorphism.
#+end proof

#+begin proof Switching criterion
The switching criterion follows immediately from the previous two lemmas.
#+end proof

# Center / centroid / idempotent 分解骨架

#+begin proposition Equal-space multilinear-form idempotent decomposition
Let

$$
\Theta:V\times V\times V\to k
$$

be a non-degenerate trilinear form.

Define the center algebra

$$
Z(V,\Theta)
=
\{
\phi\in \operatorname{End}(V)
:
\Theta(\phi x,y,z)
=
\Theta(x,\phi y,z)
=
\Theta(x,y,\phi z)
\text{ for all } x,y,z\in V
\}.
$$

Then:

1. $Z(V,\Theta)$ is a commutative algebra;
2. complete systems of pairwise orthogonal idempotents

   $$
   e_1,\dots,e_s
   $$

   in $Z(V,\Theta)$ correspond to direct-sum decompositions of $(V,\Theta)$;
3. primitive idempotent decompositions correspond to indecomposable decompositions;
4. hence the indecomposable decomposition is unique up to permutation, under the relevant hypotheses.
#+end proposition

#+begin proof Sketch
The proof has three conceptual steps.

Step 1: commutativity.

If $\phi,\psi\in Z(V,\Theta)$, then one moves $\phi$ and $\psi$ between different tensor slots using the defining center identities. Non-degeneracy then forces

$$
\phi\psi=\psi\phi.
$$

Step 2: idempotents give direct sums.

Suppose

$$
1=e_1+\cdots+e_s
$$

is a complete family of pairwise orthogonal idempotents:

$$
e_i^2=e_i,
\qquad
e_i e_j=0
\quad
(i\neq j).
$$

Let

$$
V_i:=e_iV.
$$

Then

$$
V=V_1\oplus\cdots\oplus V_s.
$$

The center identities and orthogonality of the $e_i$ imply that mixed terms vanish. Hence this is a direct-sum decomposition of the multilinear form.

Step 3: direct sums give idempotents.

Conversely, given a direct-sum decomposition

$$
V=V_1\oplus\cdots\oplus V_s
$$

with all mixed terms vanishing, the projections

$$
e_i:V\to V_i\hookrightarrow V
$$

belong to $Z(V,\Theta)$ and form a complete family of pairwise orthogonal idempotents.

Finally, primitive idempotents correspond to indecomposable summands. Since primitive-idempotent decompositions in finite-dimensional commutative algebras are unique up to permutation under the relevant hypotheses, the corresponding indecomposable direct-sum decomposition is also unique up to permutation.
#+end proof

#+begin remark General Segre-format tensor version
For a general tensor

$$
T\in V_1\otimes V_2\otimes V_3
$$

or higher-order Segre / Segre--Veronese format, one should use the centroid rather than the equal-space center.

The modern centroid theory gives the corresponding statement:

1. the centroid is a commutative algebra;
2. refined direct-sum decompositions are controlled by primitive idempotents or maximal ideals;
3. $T$ is indecomposable if and only if its centroid is local.

This is the structural basis of the idempotent route for switch detection.
#+end remark

# 算法、归约与复杂性分析

#+begin definition Three TA oracle models
We distinguish three oracle models.

1. $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$:

   Input $T$ and return all elements of

   $$
   \operatorname{Aut}(T).
   $$

2. $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$:

   Input $T$ and return a generating set for

   $$
   \operatorname{Aut}(T).
   $$

3. $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$:

   Input $T$ and answer whether

   $$
   \operatorname{Aut}(T)
   $$

   contains a non-identity element.
#+end definition

#+begin proposition Reduction using $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$
Over a finite field, $TI$ can be solved using a single query to $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ plus polynomial-time post-processing.

Given an instance $(A,B)$, first restrict $A$ and $B$ to their minimal support spaces, construct

$$
S:=A\oplus B,
$$

query $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ on $S$, and then check whether the returned group contains a switching automorphism.
#+end proposition

#+begin proof
Correctness follows from the switching criterion:

$$
A\cong B
\Longleftrightarrow
A\oplus B
\text{ contains a switching automorphism}.
$$

Since $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ returns every element of

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(S),
$$

we can inspect each returned triple

$$
(P,Q,R)
$$

and test whether it satisfies

$$
P(U_A)=U_B,
\qquad
P(U_B)=U_A,
$$

$$
Q(V_A)=V_B,
\qquad
Q(V_B)=V_A,
$$

and

$$
R(W_A)=W_B,
\qquad
R(W_B)=W_A.
$$

Thus $A\cong B$ if and only if one of the returned automorphisms is a switch.

The construction of $S$ is polynomial-time linear algebra. The post-processing is polynomial in the input size and the oracle output size.
#+end proof

#+begin warning
Calling this a bare many-one reduction is potentially misleading, because after receiving the group output we still perform a property check.

A safer wording is:

$$
TI
\text{ is solvable with one } TA_{\mathrm{all}}
\text{ query plus polynomial-time post-processing}.
$$

If the target problem is formulated as “return the automorphism group”, then this is an oracle/Turing-style reduction rather than a yes/no many-one reduction.
#+end warning

#+begin proposition Reduction using $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$
Over a finite field, if the $TA$ oracle returns a generating set for

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(T),
$$

then $TI$ is reducible to $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ with additional group-theoretic post-processing.

More precisely, this should be stated as a polynomial-time Turing reduction, not as a direct many-one reduction to a yes/no problem.
#+end proposition

#+begin proof
As before, given $(A,B)$, restrict to minimal support spaces and construct

$$
S:=A\oplus B.
$$

Query the oracle and obtain a generating set

$$
\Gamma\subseteq \operatorname{Aut}(S).
$$

The switching criterion says that

$$
A\cong B
$$

if and only if

$$
\langle \Gamma\rangle
$$

contains a switching automorphism.

The key caveat is that $\Gamma$ itself need not contain such an element. Therefore it is incorrect to merely scan the returned generators.

Instead, one must solve a group-theoretic detection problem:

$$
\text{Does the group } \langle \Gamma\rangle
\text{ contain an element sending }
(U_A,V_A,W_A)
\text{ to }
(U_B,V_B,W_B)?
$$

There are two possible routes.

Route A: matrix-group / transporter route.

View the problem as a transporter problem for the action of

$$
\langle \Gamma\rangle
$$

on triples of subspaces

$$
(U_A,U_B),
\qquad
(V_A,V_B),
\qquad
(W_A,W_B).
$$

Over finite fields, standard finite matrix-group and permutation-group machinery can be used for this type of post-processing, provided the required constructive membership and orbit/transporter subroutines are available.

Route B: centroid / idempotent route.

Compute the centroid or center algebra controlling the refined direct-sum decomposition. Then reduce switch detection to the induced action on primitive idempotents or direct-sum blocks.

This route is particularly clean under an indecomposable-summand promise. In the general case, repeated isomorphism types and subset transport must be handled carefully.

Thus the reduction is valid at the structural level, but the theorem statement must say:

$$
\text{the generated group contains a switch},
$$

not

$$
\text{the returned generating set contains a switch}.
$$
#+end proof

#+begin proposition $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ is generally insufficient
The decision oracle $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$, which only answers whether

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(T)
$$

is nontrivial, is generally insufficient for reducing $TI$ to $TA$.
#+end proposition

#+begin proof
Let

$$
S=A\oplus B.
$$

Even if

$$
A\not\cong B,
$$

it may happen that

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(A)\neq \{1\}
$$

or

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(B)\neq \{1\}.
$$

In that case,

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(S)\neq \{1\}
$$

even though no switching automorphism exists.

The oracle $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ would answer “yes, there is a nontrivial automorphism,” but this nontrivial automorphism may be internal to $A$ or $B$, rather than a switch between the two summands.

Therefore $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ cannot distinguish

$$
\text{internal symmetry}
$$

from

$$
\text{summand-switching symmetry}.
$$

So $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ is too weak in general.
#+end proof

#+begin remark Restricted positive result under rigidity
Suppose we restrict to rigid indecomposable promise instances such that

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(A)=\{1\},
\qquad
\operatorname{Aut}(B)=\{1\},
$$

and there are no repeated isomorphism-type summands causing extra permutation symmetries.

Then

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(A\oplus B)
$$

is nontrivial if and only if it contains a switching automorphism.

Under this promise, $TI$ can be reduced to $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$.

But this is not the general case. Turning arbitrary instances into rigid instances would require an additional rigidification gadget.
#+end remark

| 模型 | 是否足以完成一般 $TI\to TA$ | 原因 |
|---|---|---|
| $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ | 可以 | 整群已知，直接检测是否存在 switch。 |
| $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ | 可以，但要后处理 | 要解 transporter / orbit / membership 型群算法子问题。 |
| $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ | 一般不可以 | 非平凡 automorphism 可能来自内部对称，不一定来自 switch。 |

#+begin proposition Complexity estimate
Let $N$ denote the input size.

1. Minimal support reduction is a linear-algebraic computation and takes time polynomial in $N$.

2. Constructing

   $$
   S=A\oplus B
   $$

   also takes polynomial time.

3. With $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$, the post-processing time is polynomial in the input size and in the oracle output length.

4. With $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ over a finite field, the remaining switch detection problem can be phrased as a finite matrix-group / transporter problem. The precise polynomial-time claim depends on the available constructive membership and transporter algorithms.

5. With the centroid / idempotent route, over finite fields one may use finite-dimensional algebra algorithms to compute primitive idempotents. These are typically randomized polynomial-time in the standard models.
#+end proposition

#+begin remark Finite fields versus infinite fields
The structural theorems and the switching criterion do not depend on the field being finite.

However, the algorithmic interpretation of “returning generators for $\operatorname{Aut}(T)$” is much cleaner over finite fields.

Over a finite field $F_q$,

$$
\operatorname{GL}_n(F_q)
$$

is finite, so

$$
\operatorname{Aut}(T)
$$

is a finite group and matrix-group methods apply naturally.

Over infinite fields, $\operatorname{Aut}(T)$ may be infinite and may be better treated as an algebraic group. In that setting, a finite generating set in the ordinary finite-group sense is generally not the right output model.

Therefore the paper should separate:

1. Structural layer: arbitrary fields, subject to the hypotheses of the relevant decomposition theorem.

2. Algorithmic layer: finite fields, unless an explicit algebraic-group oracle model is introduced.
#+end remark

| 底域 | 结构性结论 | 算法性结论 |
|---|---|---|
| finite field $F_q$ | minimal support、switching criterion、centroid / direct-sum 结构都成立。 | 最适合写成 polynomial-time oracle reduction；Rónyai / matrix-group routines 可接上。 |
| effective field of characteristic $0$ | 结构通常仍成立。 | 若要算法化，需要额外规定 factorization、finite-vs-infinite group handling、algebraic-group representation。 |
| arbitrary field | 主 iff 与 minimal support 内在性成立。 | 若 oracle 模型不加强，通常只能保留结构定理，不宜贸然声称统一多项式时间算法。 |

#+begin summary Recommended theorem wording
The safest final theorem wording should be split into three layers.

Layer 1: structural statement.

$$
A\cong B
\Longleftrightarrow
A\oplus B
\text{ has a switching automorphism}.
$$

Layer 2: finite-field oracle statement.

Over finite fields, $TI$ is solvable using $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ or $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ plus polynomial-time post-processing, with the $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ case requiring group-theoretic switch detection.

Layer 3: decision-oracle limitation.

The decision oracle $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ is not sufficient in general. It works only under rigidity promises or after an additional rigidification construction.
#+end summary

This is the most urgent wording bug.

The following statement is false in general:

\text{The oracle returns generators, hence one of them is a switch.}
$$

The correct statement is:

$$
\text{The group generated by the returned generators contains a switch.}
$$

Thus scanning the generator list is insufficient.
#+end warning

If the base field is not fixed, one should not casually assume that

$$

has a useful finite generating set as an ordinary group.

Over finite fields, this is natural.

Over infinite fields, the automorphism object is often an algebraic group, and the output model must be stated differently.

Symmetric tensors and alternating tensors use diagonal actions and may require additional characteristic assumptions.

They should appear as remarks or future directions unless treated separately.

# 文中直接可引用的定理陈述草稿

#+begin theorem Main structural theorem draft
Let

$$
A\in U_A\otimes V_A\otimes W_A,
\qquad
B\in U_B\otimes V_B\otimes W_B
$$

be third-order tensors over a field $k$.

Assume both tensors are viewed on their minimal support spaces, and define

$$
S:=A\oplus B.
$$

Then the following are equivalent:

1. $A$ and $B$ are isomorphic under the action of

   $$
   \operatorname{GL}(U)\times \operatorname{GL}(V)\times \operatorname{GL}(W).
   $$

2. The automorphism group

   $$
   \operatorname{Aut}(S)
   $$

   contains a switching automorphism relative to the two-block decomposition

   $$
   S=A\oplus B.
   $$

Moreover:

(a) If a $TA$ oracle returns the whole group $\operatorname{Aut}(S)$, then over finite fields one can directly test whether a switch exists.

(b) If a $TA$ oracle returns generators for $\operatorname{Aut}(S)$, then over finite fields one can test whether the generated group contains a switch using additional matrix-group / transporter post-processing.

(c) If a $TA$ oracle only decides whether $\operatorname{Aut}(S)$ is nontrivial, then the above reduction does not work in general.
#+end theorem

#+begin proposition Recommended algorithmic wording
This paper interprets “reducing $TI$ to $TA$” as follows.

Structural layer:

construct

$$
(A,B)\mapsto S=A\oplus B
$$

and use the switching criterion.

Algorithmic layer:

depending on the $TA$ oracle model, add the corresponding group-theoretic post-processing.

Thus:

1. for $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$, one query plus direct switch detection suffices;
2. for $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$, one query plus generated-group switch detection suffices;
3. for $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$, the reduction fails in general, except under rigidity promises or additional gadget constructions.
#+end proposition

#+begin remark Suggested related-work wording
The tensor and bilinear-map language follows Strassen's framework. The $TI$-complete complexity perspective follows Grochow--Qiao and subsequent work. The complexity background for ring isomorphism, automorphism, and cubic-form equivalence follows Kayal--Saxena and Agrawal--Saxena. The direct-sum / centroid / refined decomposition theory is informed by the center algebra theory for multilinear forms and the recent centroid theory for Segre-format tensors. Algorithmically, the finite-dimensional algebra and primitive-idempotent route is connected to Rónyai-type algorithms and their descendants.
#+end remark

# 三分钟组会口头版

#+begin summary Three-minute meeting summary
The cleanest structure is the following.

First, we should not say simply “$TI$ reduces to $TA$” until we specify what $TA$ returns.

There are three versions:

1. return the full automorphism group;
2. return generators;
3. only decide whether a nontrivial automorphism exists.

The core construction is:

$$
S:=A\oplus B.
$$

After restricting $A$ and $B$ to their minimal support spaces, we have the structural equivalence

$$
A\cong B
\Longleftrightarrow
S
\text{ has a switching automorphism}.
$$

This iff is easy and does not need Krull--Schmidt.

The difficulty is detecting switch from the oracle output.

If the oracle returns the whole group, we can inspect the group.

If the oracle returns generators, we must check whether the generated group contains a switch. We cannot claim that the returned generating set itself contains one.

If the oracle only decides whether there is a nontrivial automorphism, then it is generally too weak, because internal automorphisms of $A$ or $B$ cause false positives.

Therefore the safe theorem statement is:

Over finite fields, $TI$ reduces to $TA_{\mathrm{all}}$ or $TA_{\mathrm{gen}}$ with polynomial-time post-processing. The $TA_{\mathrm{dec}}$ version fails in general unless we add rigidity assumptions or gadgets.
#+end summary
